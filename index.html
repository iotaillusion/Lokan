Exploring Safer Home AI

Optivize shares design concepts for privacy-preserving, auditable AI in home environments. The focus is research and prototypes, not product commitments.

Research Areas

We explore approaches to integrate AI into the home while minimizing data exposure and maximizing user control.

Local Sensing

Prototypes of cameras and sensors that run detection locally and only log structured events, reducing the need for cloud transmission.

  • Research on edge inference efficiency
  • Methods for redacting sensitive regions
  • Experiments with encrypted storage

Context-Aware Energy

Models that simulate occupancy and environment to suggest energy use patterns, without exposing raw schedules to external servers.

  • Adaptive thermostat simulations
  • Local prediction of peak load
  • Open data experiments for validation

Safety & Privacy Principles

Our guidelines are experimental and meant to inspire discussion on what “safe home AI” should look like.

Design Goals

  • Run as much processing locally as feasible
  • Give users readable logs of automated decisions
  • Make opt-out and shutdown always available

Ongoing Questions

  • Best ways to verify models on-device
  • Balancing energy savings with privacy
  • How to make external audits trustworthy
